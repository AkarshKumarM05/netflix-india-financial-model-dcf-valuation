Investment Thesis

  • ✅

    Premium Segment Leadership

    19% market share with highest ARPU (₹1,496/month FY25E). Strong brand loyalty in affluent India despite mass-market competition.

  • ✅

    Operating Leverage

    EBIT margins expanded from 1.6% (FY21) to 9.8% (FY25E) — 41.13% Revenue CAGR outpacing content amortization.

  • ✅

    FCF Inflection Point

    Reached positive Free Cash Flow in FY24. Declining parent capital contributions (₹450 Cr FY21 → ₹80 Cr FY25E) confirms self-sufficiency.

Key Risks

  • ⚠️

    Competitive Consolidation

    JioCinema + Disney+ Hotstar merger controls cricket/sports rights (23% share). Amazon Prime Video aggressive bundling.

  • ⚠️

    Content Cost Inflation

    Content spending at 52.5% of revenue (₹1,980 Cr FY25E). Rising original Indian content production costs.

  • ⚠️

    Regulatory & Currency

    Potential digital services tax changes, content censorship risks, and INR depreciation impacting parent company reporting.

Historical Analysis · FY2021–FY2025E

The Growth Story

Netflix India achieved a 41.13% Revenue CAGR (FY21–FY25E) and 56.5% Subscriber CAGR. The FY24 subscriber explosion (+142.9% YoY to 17M) reflects successful bundling partnerships with Jio and Airtel, compressing ARPU from ₹2,781 to ₹1,395 while dramatically expanding the subscriber base.

OTT Market Share — India (Q3 2025)

Netflix holds 19% share — premium positioning vs. Jio Hotstar (23%) and Prime Video (23%).

FY24 Subscriber Explosion

Subscribers jumped from 7M to 17M (+142.9% YoY) driven by telecom bundling (Jio, Airtel, Vodafone) and the ₹149 mobile-only plan launch.

Ad-Tier Revenue Stream

Ad-supported tier launched generating ₹225 Cr in FY25E (6% of revenue), growing 40%+ annually. Projected ₹2,000+ Cr by FY2030.

Content Efficiency Improving

Content costs declining as % of revenue: 57.9% (FY21) → 52.5% (FY25E). 28 original Indian titles launched in 2025.
